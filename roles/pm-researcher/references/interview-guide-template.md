# Interview Guide Template

> Purpose: discovery / problem / usability / concept-validation / exit
> Audience: {specific persona, e.g. "PM at Korean B2B SaaS companies, 2-5 yrs experience"}
> Interviewer: {@name}
> Duration: 30-45 min

---

## Pre-interview

- Record (with permission)
- Take sparse notes in the moment — rely on recording for synthesis
- Have a co-interviewer if possible (one to talk, one to observe)
- Reconfirm audience persona fit in the first 2 min

## Warm-up (2 min)

Low-stakes, not about the research topic. Build rapport.

- "Thanks for making time. How's your week been?"
- "Tell me a bit about your current role."

## Context setting (3-5 min)

Establish the **recent concrete situation** that will anchor the rest of
the interview. Avoid hypotheticals.

- "Walk me through the last time you did {the thing we care about}."
- "When was that? What were you trying to accomplish?"

## The Job (10-15 min) — non-leading, open-ended

Do NOT ask about features. Ask about the Job.

- "What were you trying to do, fundamentally?"
- "What would 'done' look like?"
- "Why did this matter right then?"

Probe:
- Time pressure? ("Did you have a deadline?")
- Other people involved? ("Who else needed to know / approve / contribute?")
- Cost of not doing it? ("What would have happened if you hadn't?")

## Struggles + workarounds (10-15 min) — concrete, recent examples

- "What got in the way?"
- "Show me — what did you actually do?" (If remote, screen-share helps)
- "What did you have to work around?"
- "Have you tried any tools for this? Which? What made you stop using them?"

Probe for:
- Specific points of friction
- Emotional reactions ("I was so frustrated when...")
- Abandoned attempts (these are gold for JTBD)
- Who was watching or judging the outcome

## Ideal state (5 min)

Reframe to solution-adjacent without leading:
- "If a magic wand removed all the barriers, what would change?"
- "Describe the dream version of doing this."

Do NOT ask "would you use a product that does X?" — users are terrible
predictors of their own behavior.

## Wrap (3 min)

- "Is there anything I should have asked that I didn't?"
- "Who else should I talk to about this?"
- "Can I follow up in a week if I have a few clarifying questions?"

## Post-interview (within 30 min)

Capture in `pm-notes`:
- 3 most surprising quotes (verbatim)
- Top 2 Jobs surfaced
- Top 2 friction points
- Anything that contradicted prior interviews
- Follow-up questions for next interview

---

## Leading-question anti-patterns

Never:
- "Would you use a feature that does X?" — hypothetical, users lie
- "Do you think X is important?" — they will say yes to be polite
- "How often do you wish you could do X?" — leads to fabrication

Prefer:
- "Tell me about the last time you did X." — concrete
- "Walk me through what happened next." — narrative
- "What did you try before / instead?" — reveals true priority
