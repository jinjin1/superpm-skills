# Pivot Assessment — B2C to B2B2C

> Strategic question: should we pivot our consumer app "CalendarAI" to a
> B2B2C model (sold to HR teams for employees)?

## Recommendation

**Do NOT pivot.** Sharpen the consumer wedge instead. Confidence: Medium.

## Why (ranked)

1. **Strongest reason**: Our retention is already at 38% Day-30 on the
   consumer product — top quartile for the category. The growth bottleneck
   is acquisition cost, not product quality. B2B2C solves a distribution
   problem but surrenders the product-market fit signal we've found.

2. **Structural reason**: B2B2C forces us to serve two Jobs simultaneously
   — the HR buyer's "prove value to employees" Job, and the employee's
   "manage my calendar better" Job. Selling to HR means product roadmap
   bends toward reporting / compliance / SSO, not the core Job. That
   weakens consumer retention further over 12 months.

3. **Competitive reason**: Three well-funded B2B2C startups already own
   the HR channel (Calendly Enterprise, Clockwise, Reclaim). Moving there
   puts us in their fight, not ours. Consumer calendar AI still has only
   2 meaningful players.

## Confidence: Medium

- What would raise it to High: 10+ user interviews with HR buyers showing
  they would pay; right now we have only 3 exec conversations (warm)
- What would drop it to Low: evidence that consumer CAC is structurally
  unfixable (next 90 days of growth experiments failing)

## Key risks (if we DO pivot)

1. **Product dilution**: B2B requirements pull us into roadmap items
   (admin console, SSO, audit log) worth 40-50% of eng capacity for 6
   months. Risk of losing consumer edge.
   - Early warning: consumer Day-30 retention drops below 32% within 90
     days of pivot kickoff.

2. **Unit economics surprise**: B2B2C lands a contract once, then we owe
   support indefinitely. Our current team (6 people) cannot sustain
   enterprise support SLAs.
   - Early warning: first B2B customer escalation that takes > 48 hours
     to resolve.

## If this is wrong

Reverse conditions:
- Consumer growth plateaus at the current MAU for 6+ months despite
  aggressive paid + referral experiments
- HR buyers start asking for us proactively (inbound > 10 qualified leads
  per month)
- B2B2C competitors show signs of weakness (churn spikes, pricing cuts)

If 2 of these 3 hold, revisit.

## Next evidence needed

- 10 HR buyer interviews (not exec intros — real buyer committee members):
  `pm-researcher` to design + run
- Consumer CAC breakdown: what's actually driving cost growth?
  `pm-analyst` to dissect
- Kill criteria for the current consumer strategy, if we decided to
  sharpen: `pm-critic` pre-mortem

## Framework applied

- **10x vs 2x**: A successful consumer play at our current momentum is a
  10-30x outcome (calendar AI category). B2B2C is a 2-3x outcome (commodity
  segment with 3 competitors). Math favors sharpening consumer.
- **Focus as subtraction**: Adding B2B2C is addition disguised as focus.
  Saying no to pivot is the real focus move.
